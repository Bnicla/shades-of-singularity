When AI composes symphonies, writes novels, and generates scientific hypotheses at superhuman quality and infinite volume, the cultural meaning of human creative expression enters crisis. Three responses are already visible: denial (insisting AI cannot "truly" create); integration (AI as creative partner); and retreat (provably human-made art valued for its limitation and mortality). Each has historical precedent. The Arts and Crafts movement responded similarly to industrialization. Photography did not kill painting; it redefined what painting was for.

Unmanaged, this is a cultural loss (-1) rather than catastrophe: a devaluation of human creative work in economic terms, a saturation of cultural channels with generated content, a gradual erosion of the apprenticeship structures that develop artistic skill. Governed, it becomes +3: AI democratizes creative tools, enabling billions who never had access to training or studios to express themselves. The governed future is one where the means of creation become universal while the meaning of creation evolves.

The adversarial point: creative AI may simply be another tool in a long history of creative tools. The synthesizer did not destroy music. Photoshop did not destroy visual art. The panic about AI creativity may be another round of a perennial moral panic about technology and authenticity that resolves through cultural adaptation.

This is probably right in the long run. The short-run disruption is real: working artists face economic displacement now, and cultural adaptation takes a generation.

**Key tension:** The economic disruption of creative workers is immediate. The cultural adaptation is generational.
